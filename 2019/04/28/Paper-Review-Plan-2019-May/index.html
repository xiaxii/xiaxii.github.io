While significant progress has been made to build reliable social sensing system, some important challenges have not been well addressed yet.

  1. Existing social sensing systems did not fully solve the dynamic truth estimation problem where the ground truth of claims changes over time.
  2. Many current solutions are not scalable to large-scale social sensing events because of the centralized nature of their data analytics algorithms.
  3. The transition of true values of measured variables is constrained by some physical rules that must be followed to ensure correct estimation.

In this project, we developed a scalable streaming social sensing system with explicit considerations of the physical constraints on the measured variables to address the above challenges. We evaluated our framework through real world social sensing applications. The evaluation results show that our system is scalable and outperforms the state-of-the-art solutions in terms of both effectiveness and efficiency. The results have been published in IEEE ICDCS 2017, IEEE BigData 2017, IEEE Transanction on Big Data.
